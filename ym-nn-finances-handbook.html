01

Where NNs Can Keep Money — The 3 Accounts

Every NN has access to three distinct account types. Each has its own purpose, rules, and tracking requirements. All three appear on your NN's Finance Dashboard.

National

YM Bank Account

Funds held in the national YM bank account. Managed centrally by Finance. Used for larger sums — fundraisers, event registrations, donations. This money is NOT physically with you.

Local Cash

NN Cashbox

Physical cash kept in a secure lockbox. For small, day-to-day needs like dues and bake sales. Maximum $200 at any time. Only NNC & Finance Lead have access.

Personal ★★★

NNC Personal Bank

NNC's personal checking account used temporarily for YM funds. Must be completely separate from personal money. Avoid interest accrual on these funds.

Cash Limit: No NN should hold more than $200 in local cash at any time. Anything beyond this must be deposited into the National account immediately. All cash — even if kept locally — MUST be logged via JotForm.
Cash vs. National — Key Differences
Local Cash (Cashbox)
  • Kept on hand locally
  • Small amounts only (max $200)
  • Day-to-day: dues, bake sales
  • Must still be logged via JotForm
  • Physically secured in lockbox
YM National Bank
  • Managed at national level
  • Larger sums, fundraisers, donations
  • Requires JotForm to access funds
  • Officially recorded, full oversight
  • Reimbursed via Venmo or Zelle
02

When to Submit the JotForm

Rule of Thumb: If money is moved in any way — you must submit the JotForm. No exceptions. This includes cash kept locally.
YM Email Required: JotForm submissions must be made using your YM email (e.g. name@ymsisters.com). Submissions made with personal emails will NOT be accepted. Dashboard access will not be granted if requested from a personal email.

1 — Income Received

Any time your NN receives money: dues, bake sales, fundraising, donations, merch sales.

2 — Expense Incurred

Any time your NN spends money: supplies, food, decor, transportation, honorariums.

3 — Internal Transfer

Moving funds between your own 3 accounts. See Section 05 for full detail and important distinctions.

Important: Submitting a form does NOT automatically move or release funds. All updates appear on the dashboard only after Finance Department approval.

04

Logging Expenses

When your NN spends money, log the expense via JotForm. There are two types of expense submissions depending on how reimbursement will happen.

Option A

Request Reimbursement from YM Bank

Someone paid out of pocket. Request reimbursement from YM Bank. Finance reimburses via Venmo or Zelle.

  • Total Expense Amount
  • Expense Category (see table below)
  • Expense Details — date, event, subcategory
  • Upload Receipt(s) — required
  • Itemized Details — item names only
  • Reimbursement Method: Venmo or Zelle
Option B

Log Already-Reimbursed Expense

Person was already paid back using on-hand cash or personal funds. ONLY to record it on the dashboard — no funds move.

  • Total Expense Amount
  • Expense Category
  • Expense Details
  • Upload Receipt(s) — required
  • Itemized Details
  • Which fund was used: Cash Box or Personal Bank
Expense Details Format
Format: Date · Event Name · Expense Subcategory
Examples:  1/9/26 NJ Gala – Decorations  |  3/15/26 Ramadan Iftar – Food  |  4/20/26 Spring Retreat – Transportation
Itemized Details — How to Fill In
What to include: List the item names purchased. Do not include individual prices. Do not list tax as a line item.

Example: Balloons, Table cloth, String lights, Streamers, Paper plates
Tax is covered by YM. Include the full receipt total (including tax) in your Total Expense Amount. Just don't list tax separately in the itemized section.
Receipt Requirements
No Receipt = No Approval. If there is no receipt attached to your submission, it will not be approved — no exceptions.

Amazon Purchases

No screenshots. Download the receipt as a PDF from your Amazon account and upload the PDF file.

Any Online Purchase

Upload as a PDF — not a screenshot. Download the order confirmation from the site as a PDF.

Zelle / Venmo to Vendor

Screenshot is fine for Zelle or Venmo transfers made to an external vendor.

Paper Receipts

The entire receipt must be clearly and fully visible. Blurry, cut off, or incomplete paper receipts will NOT be approved.

No Personal Purchases on YM Receipts. YM expenses must be purchased completely separately. Each YM purchase must be on its own receipt with no personal items included.

05

Transferring Money

Internal Transfer — What It Actually Is
Many NNs are selecting "Internal Transfer" incorrectly. Please read this carefully before selecting this transaction type on the JotForm.

Internal Transfer IS:

Moving money between your team's own existing YM accounts. Valid examples:

  • YM Bank → Cash Box
  • Cash Box → YM Bank
  • Personal Bank Account → YM Bank
  • YM Bank → Personal Bank Account

Internal Transfer is NOT:

If an individual is depositing their own personal money into a YM account for the first time — that is Income to Personal Bank Account, not an Internal Transfer.

Internal Transfer = moving existing YM funds between your own accounts.
New personal money coming in = Income.
JotForm Fields for Internal Transfer
  • Transaction Type: Internal Transfer
  • Total Transfer Amount
  • From account: YM Bank / Cash Box / Personal Bank Account
  • To account: YM Bank / Cash Box / Personal Bank Account
  • Transfer Description / Reason — e.g. $15 from dues, $30 from merch sales
NN-to-NN Transfer (Collab Events)

Transferring Funds to Another NN

When two NNs collaborate on an event and split costs, each NN submits their own separate JotForm to reflect how the transfer affects their own dashboard.

The NN Paying (Richardson)

  • Transaction Type: Expense
  • Amount: $25
  • Category: NN-NN Transfer
  • From: YM Bank

The NN Receiving (Murphy)

  • Transaction Type: Income
  • Amount: $25
  • Category: NN-NN Transfer
  • To: YM Bank

Example: Murphy spent $50 on pizza for a joint event. Richardson owes Murphy $25 for their half. Both NNs submit separately as shown above.

06

Cash Handling Guidelines

Storage Requirements

  • Keep all cash in a secure safe or lockbox
  • Only NNC and Finance Lead may have access
  • Cash must never be mixed with personal money
  • If using a bank account, use a dedicated section for NN funds only
  • Ensure funds do not accrue interest
  • Recommended: send excess to YM National Account for storage

Receipts & Documentation

  • Always request and keep receipts for every purchase
  • Photograph receipts immediately after purchase in case physical copy is lost
  • Submit receipts with every expense JotForm (see Section 04 for format rules)
  • Do not reimburse from collected cash before logging — all funds must be logged first
  • Report total income AND total expenses separately for every event
Event Reporting Rule: Report the total income for the event (dues, sales, donations) AND the total expenses (all supplies, decor, food, etc.) separately. Do not deduct expenses from income first — all funds must be logged completely before any deductions.

If DeniedYou receive a follow-up email with reason
Reasons for Denial

No receipt attached, or missing bank statement
Receipt is not itemized or not clearly visible in full
Amazon or online purchase submitted as a screenshot instead of a PDF
Personal items present on the same receipt as YM purchases

Pre-Approved Lump Sum

A Pre-Approved Lump Sum is a cash request submitted without receipts upfront — when your NN needs cash before an event. Requires dual approval before funds are released.

Process
1

Submit Request via JotForm

Include: Requested Amount, Purpose of Funds, and Approximate Use breakdown of where the funding will go

2

Dual Approval Required

Must be approved by both: Regional Finance Rep / SRC AND National Finance Team

3

Funds Sent via Venmo

Once approved, funds are sent through Venmo. Dashboard reflects the amount and details provided.

4

Submit Lump Sum Report — Within 14 Days

Breakdown of how funds were used, event name, and leftover balance.

5

Return Unused Funds — Within 7 Business Days

Any unused funds must be returned to YM Finance via Venmo @Young-Muslims within 7 business days after report submission.

Approved Uses
  • Venue costs
  • Event supplies
  • Food
  • Speaker honorariums
Funds may only be used for YM-related purposes. Misuse of lump sum funds is an audit flag and will result in a formal review.
10

NN Audits

The National Finance Team conducts audits on a rolling basis every few months. Every NNC/NN Shura member is responsible for keeping their dashboard fully up to date at all times.

What Gets Reviewed
1

Dashboard Review

Current balance, income and expenses, spending trends, and status of all JotForm submissions

2

Consultation with SRC / SR Finance Lead

Your SRC or SR Finance Lead is consulted for additional context on NN financial activity

3

Audit Check-In Call (if needed)

Finance schedules a call with NN leadership to clarify patterns and address concerns

4

Action Items & Support

Finance provides a clear path forward with specific guidance

Audit Red Flags
Overspending beyond available balance
Underspending with no clear purpose for accumulated funds
Repeated missing or incomplete documentation
Frequent denied reimbursement submissions
Inconsistencies between YM Bank, Cash Box, and Personal Bank logs
Dashboard not kept up to date
Stay Audit-Ready: Submit JotForms promptly for every transaction. Keep local cash minimal and documented. Review your dashboard regularly for accuracy.
