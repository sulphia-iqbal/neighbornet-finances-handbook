01

Where NNs Can Keep Money — The 3 Accounts

Every NN has access to three account types. All three appear on your NN's Finance Dashboard.

National

YM Bank Account

Your NN's funds held in the National YM Bank. This money is not physically with you but you can withdraw from it at any time! Think of it like your NN's checking account, where the "bank" is YM National Finance.

Local Cash

NN Cashbox

Physical cash kept in a secure cashbox. No NN should hold more than $200 in local cash at any time. Anything beyond this must be deposited into your YM Bank Account immediately.

Personal Bank Account

NNC Personal Bank

NNC's personal checking account used temporarily for YM funds. Must be completely separate from personal money.

One Rule That Applies to Every Submission

Always Log Income and Expenses Separately

For every event, submit income and expenses as separate JotForms. Log the full income total (dues, sales, donations) and the full expense total (supplies, decor, food, etc.) on their own — do not subtract one from the other first and submit only the net.

Why this matters

Separate logging gives Finance Team and your team a complete picture — how much revenue your events are generating, what you're spending on, and which income sources are working. If you only log the net, that detail disappears and it becomes impossible to see what's worth doing again or what needs to change.

Receipt Requirements

No Personal Purchases on Receipt

Each YM purchase must be on its own separate receipt. Do not include personal items on the same transaction — keep YM and personal purchases completely separate.

Finance Team Tip

If you need to make personal purchases at the same store, buy your personal items first, then make a second, separate transaction for the YM expenses. Upload only that second receipt to your JotForm.

Upload Invoice/Receipt as PDF for Online Purchases (e.g. Amazon)

Screenshots of order pages are not accepted. Download the order confirmation or invoice as a PDF, then upload the PDF as your receipt.

For Amazon orders:

  1. Sign in to Amazon
  2. Go to Returns & Orders
  3. Find your purchase and click Invoice or Order Details
  4. Select View Invoice to open the printable version
  5. Press Cmd+P (Mac) or Ctrl+P (Windows), then choose Save as PDF

Paying a Vendor via Zelle or Venmo

If you paid a vendor directly through Zelle or Venmo (e.g. a calligrapher, a rental company, a caterer), always ask them for an invoice first. If they don't have one — for example, if the arrangement was made informally over WhatsApp — a screenshot of the payment confirmation is accepted as your receipt.

Paper Receipts Must Be Fully Visible

Take a clear photo of the entire receipt — all items, prices, and the total must be readable. If the receipt is long, lay it flat and capture it in full. Blurry or cut-off photos will not be approved.

05

Pre-Approved Lump Sum

A Pre-Approved Lump Sum is a cash request submitted without receipts upfront — when your NN needs cash before an event to cover venue costs, event supplies, food, or speaker honorariums. Requires dual approval from Finance Team and your SR before funds are released.

Process
1

Email Finance Team to Request Approval

Send an email to finance@ymsisters.com and CC your SR Finance Lead (may be your SRC) explaining what the lump sum is for and how much you need. Wait for approval from both Finance Team and your SR before moving forward.

2

Submit Internal Transfer + Venmo/Zelle Request

Once approved, submit an Internal Transfer JotForm (YM Bank → Personal Bank Account) to log the movement on your dashboard — then send a Venmo request to @Young-Muslims or Zelle to finance@ymsisters.com for the approved amount to receive the actual funds. See Section 06a.

3

Submit Lump Sum Report — Within 14 Days

Reply in the same email thread with a breakdown of how the funds were used, the event name, and any leftover balance.

4

Return Unused Funds — Within 7 Business Days

Any unused funds must be returned within 7 business days after report submission. Submit an Internal Transfer JotForm (Personal Bank Account → YM Bank) to log the return, then send the funds via Venmo to @Young-Muslims or Zelle to finance@ymsisters.com.

06

Transfers

Any time money moves between accounts — whether within your own team, between two NNs, or between Sisters and Brothers — you need to log it. There are three types of transfer submissions, each covered in its own sub-section.

06a

Internal Transfer

Money moving between your own team's accounts — Cash Box, YM Bank, or NNC Personal Bank. The money is already in your YM system; you're just moving it from one pocket to another.

06b

NN-to-NN Transfer

Money moving between your NN and another NN for a collab event. Up to 3 JotForms are required to correctly reflect the split on both teams' dashboards.

06c

Sisters ↔ Brothers Settlement

Money moving between a YM Sisters NN and a YM Brothers chapter for a joint event. Only the Sisters side submits JotForms.

06a

Internal Transfer

Many NNs select "Internal Transfer" incorrectly. Read this carefully before selecting this transaction type on the JotForm.

Internal Transfer IS:

Moving money between your team's own existing YM accounts. The money is already in your YM system — you're just moving it from one pocket to another.

  • YM Bank → Cash Box
  • Cash Box → YM Bank
  • Personal Bank Account → YM Bank
  • YM Bank → Personal Bank Account
Example: Your cashbox has $40 and you want to transfer it to the YM Bank. Submit an Internal Transfer for Cash Box → YM Bank for $40.

Internal Transfer is NOT:

When brand new money arrives to your NNC — that's Income, not an Internal Transfer.

Example: Your NNC was sent $50 for event dues via Zelle for this past event. That's new money entering your YM system — submit an Income JotForm. You have two options for where to log it:

Log to Personal Bank Account — if your NNC wants to keep the funds in their personal bank account like it is right now.

Log to YM Bank (recommended) — if you want to send it straight to nationals right away. Follow the steps in Section 03 — If you received a digital payment (Zelle / Venmo).

07

Cash on Hand — Rules & Responsibilities

Storage Requirements

  • Keep all cash in a secure safe or lockbox — consider this one
  • Only NNC and NN Finance Lead may have access
  • Cash must never be mixed with personal money
  • NNs may hold a maximum of $200 cash on hand. Anything above $200 should be sent to your YM Bank Account via Internal Transfer (see Section 06a)
  • SRs may not hold cash on hand — all funds must go to the YM Bank Account

Why the $200 Limit Matters

NN funds are an amanah — money that the community has trusted you to protect and manage responsibly.

The more cash you keep locally, the greater the risk of it being lost, stolen, misplaced, or mixed up with personal money. Once cash is in your possession, you are responsible for keeping it safe and accounting for every dollar.

What to do

Once the cashbox reaches $200, send the extra funds to the YM Bank through an Internal Transfer. The money is safer there, easier to track, and frees you of the liability.

When to Use Cash for Reimbursements

Only use cash when you can give the person the exact amount they are owed. This usually works best for simple, whole-dollar amounts.

Why cash reimbursements can be tricky

Many purchases include cents. For example, imagine you have $150 in your cashbox and someone needs to be reimbursed $19.75. It may be difficult to give them the exact amount in cash, which can make it tempting to round the reimbursement up to a cleaner number.

Even though the difference may seem small, that is not proper accounting. If someone is owed $19.75, they should receive exactly $19.75 — not $20.

This is also part of handling the community's money with proper Islamic financial etiquette.

For this reason, reimbursements should usually be made through the YM Bank account, where you can send the exact amount down to the cent. Cash should only be used when the reimbursement amount is simple, exact, and easy to document — which in practice is rare.

Understanding Your Dashboard

All NNCs and their NN Shuras have commentator access to their NN's Finance Dashboards in Google Sheets. This dashboard tracks and analyzes your NN's financial activity in real time, reflecting what has been submitted via JotForm.

Dashboard Tabs

Dashboard Tab

The overview tab. Shows your total income, total expenses, net balance (YTD), and transaction count. Also includes your top 3 expenses and income entries, top spending months, a quarterly breakdown, and charts for income vs. expenses by account and by category.

Year Tabs (2026, 2025, 2024…)

Every approved JotForm submission appears as a row here. This is the tab to reference when reviewing submission logs and checking account balances.

Denied Requests Tab

Shows all JotForm submissions Finance Team has denied. If your submission is denied, you will receive an email from Finance Team explaining why — check that email, correct the issue, and resubmit.

JotForm Approval Process

Step 1Fill out and submit JotForm with YM email
Step 2You'll receive an email confirmation that Finance Team has received your submission
Step 3Finance Team reviews and approves / denies daily
›
If Approved

The entry's status in your dashboard switches from Pending to Approved

If Denied

You receive a follow-up email with the reason, and the entry appears in the Denied Requests tab of your dashboard

Common Reasons for Denial
Invalid submission (incorrect form fields, wrong account type selected, personal email used)
No receipt attached, or missing bank statement
Receipt is not itemized or not clearly visible in full
Amazon or online purchase submitted as a screenshot instead of a PDF
Personal items present on the same receipt as YM purchases
10

NN Audits

The National Finance Team conducts audits on a rolling basis every quarter.

How Audits Are Conducted
1

Dashboard Review

The National Finance Team reviews your dashboard — current balance, income and expenses, spending trends, and use of JotForm — and comes with a list of questions

2

Group Chat Created

The National Finance Team creates a group chat with your NNC and your SRC / SR Finance Lead

3

Audit Call

Finance Team schedules a call with NN leadership and SR Finance Lead (optional) to go through their questions, clarify patterns, and address concerns. NN leadership also has the opportunity to ask questions and discuss strategies or ideas

4

Action Items Sent

After the call, the National Finance Team sends over action items for both NN leadership and the Finance Team based on what was discussed

Topics That May Come Up in Your Audit
Missing JotForm entries for expenses incurred or income generated from events that took place during the year
Frequent denied reimbursement submissions
Potential inconsistencies between YM Bank, Cash Box, and Personal Bank logs and the actual account balances
Underspending with no clear purpose for accumulated funds
